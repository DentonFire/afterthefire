What to Expect When You Return

Even if the fire seemed small, your home may look very different now. Firefighters often have to break windows or cut holes in the roof to release dangerous heat and smoke. We also check for hidden fire inside walls, which may require removing sheetrock.

Please be careful: Soot and dirty water left behind can contain materials that may make you sick. It is best to minimize your time inside until it has been cleaned.

üõë SAFETY FIRST

Do not enter your damaged home unless the Fire Department explicitly says it is safe.

  • Roofs and floors may be weakened.
  • Do not clean up or repair anything until the fire investigation is officially completed.
  • You may temporarily cover broken windows or holes to prevent weather damage, but do not disturb the scene.

Immediate Next Steps

üêæ Care for Your Pets

Scared animals often react by biting or scratching. Handle them gently.

Tip: Contact your vet to assess their overall health if they were exposed to smoke. Try to leave them with family or friends while you handle immediate logistics.

üîí Secure Your Property

If you must leave your home vacant, notify the police to prevent looting. You may need to board up windows/doors to secure the premises.
